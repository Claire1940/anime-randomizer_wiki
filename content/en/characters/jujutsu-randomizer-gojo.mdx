<Section icon={Swords} title="Skills That Fit the Gojo Playstyle">

The best Gojo-inspired playstyle in Jujutsu Randomizer is not about pretending the skill roll is fixed. It is about recognizing which random skills let you control space, burst quickly, and stay mobile. That means you should read your bar at spawn, then build your fight plan around stun, teleport, domain pressure, or high-damage punish tools.

<Callout type="success" title="Combat Priority">
If your roll gives you control plus burst, play aggressively. If your roll gives you mobility plus a counter, play safer and force mistakes. The kit decides the route.
</Callout>

| Skill type | Example | Why it matters | Gojo-style value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Burst damage | Hollow Purple | High damage and strong knockback | Very close to the strongest fantasy |
| Control setup | Frame Punch | Freezes targets for setup | Lets you create clean punish windows |
| Mobility burst | Instant Zap | Teleports to the enemy and lands damage | Great for pressure and surprise engages |
| Defensive tool | Simple Domain | Grants invulnerability frames | Helps you survive burst and reset spacing |
| Healing | Reverse Cursed Technique | Restores HP | Keeps you in longer rounds |
| Domain pressure | Infinite Void / Malevolent Shrine | Controls space and punishes crowds | Strong when the arena gets messy |

The cleanest pattern is usually control into burst. Frame Punch into Hollow Purple is the obvious example, but the same logic works with Angry Barrage, Dismantle, Furnace, or a teleport skill that puts you behind the target. If you roll a mobility skill, use it to create a better angle rather than gambling on a straight duel.

| Combo pattern | Why it works | Main risk |
|---|---|---|
| Frame Punch → Hollow Purple | Freeze first, then cash out damage | Missing the freeze wastes the setup |
| Instant Zap → Lucky Uppercut | Fast engage into reliable follow-up | Overcommitting into a counter |
| Boogie Woogie → Furnace | Forces bad positioning into area damage | A careless swap can backfire |
| Domain skill → burst finisher | Big-area control into a clean kill | Wasting the domain on a single escape target |

<Callout type="warning" title="Do Not Overforce">
A flashy Gojo-style combo is not helpful if the target can simply walk away or punish your cooldowns. Keep one escape option ready and only commit when the payoff is obvious.
</Callout>
