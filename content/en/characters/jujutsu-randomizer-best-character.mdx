<Section icon={Target} title="How To Judge A Roll In Live Matches">

A good roll is easy to spot once you know what to look for. In live matches, do not stare at rarity or theme alone. Check whether the passive supports your mobility, damage, and escape options before you commit to it.

<Callout type="warning" title="Fast Decision Rule">
Do not keep a roll just because it looks rare. Keep it because it helps you win the next fight, the next domain exchange, or the next escape.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Read the passive first">
    Identify whether the roll boosts damage, HP, defense, or a specific skill family. That tells you whether it is a universal pick or a niche pick.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check your current skills">
    If your kit includes blood attacks, Black Flash, weapons, or domains, look for the passive that matches that package.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Ask one survival question">
    Decide whether the roll helps you stay alive longer than your current choice. If the answer is no, the safer passive may be better.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Lock the value, not the label">
    Choose the roll that improves your fight plan. A flashy name is nice, but consistent damage and safety win more rounds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Live Match Signal | Good Sign | Bad Sign |
|---|---|---|
| Skill synergy | Passive matches current kit | Passive only helps a skill you do not have |
| Survival | More HP or less incoming damage | No help when pressured |
| Pressure | Faster punish windows | Passive is too slow to matter |
| Flexibility | Works in multiple fights | Only useful in one matchup |

If you want the cleanest habit, test the roll in this order: damage value first, survival second, then niche synergy. That gives you a stable decision even when the match starts to spiral.
