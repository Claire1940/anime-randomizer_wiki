<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Skills and Pairings for Gojo-Style Play">

The strongest Gojo-style turns usually start with a setup tool, then move into a high-damage punish. In practice, that means you want one control skill, one mobility skill, and one finisher. If the round gives you a domain or a strong counter, even better. The goal is not to press every button; the goal is to make the enemy spend theirs first.

**Recommended skill priorities**

| Skill | Role | Best use |
|---|---|---|
| **Frame Punch** | Control setup | Freeze an enemy, then cash out with burst damage |
| **Hollow Purple** | Burst finisher | Punish a stunned target or a bad dodge |
| **Instant Zap** | Gap closer | Catch a retreating player or start pressure |
| **Simple Domain** | Defense | Survive burst windows and reset the fight |
| **Reverse Cursed Technique** | Sustain | Recover after a bad trade and keep tempo |

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
A strong finisher is not the same as a strong opener. If you throw **Hollow Purple** without setup, good players simply move away and punish the cooldown.
</Callout>

| Combo path | Result | Risk level |
|---|---|---|
| Frame Punch > Hollow Purple | Clean burst window | Low |
| Instant Zap > Lucky Uppercut | Fast punish chain | Medium |
| Surprise Attack > Dismantle | Angle swap into pressure | Medium |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Match Plan">

This section is where the build becomes practical. The fastest way to improve is to stop improvising every fight and follow the same match logic each round. You check the roll, identify your win condition, then use mobility and control to create a clean punish. That approach fits the random-skill structure better than trying to force a fixed combo every time.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Read your roll immediately">
    Check whether your round gives you burst, mobility, defense, or a domain. Your first job is identifying your win condition before you sprint into combat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose the safe opener">
    If you have **Frame Punch**, a teleport, or a quick gap closer, use that to start pressure. If not, wait for a better angle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Force a reaction">
    Make the enemy dodge, block, or retreat. A Gojo-style build works best when the opponent is already moving the wrong way.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Spend your burst at the right moment">
    Use **Hollow Purple** or another big hit after control connects. Do not waste your main damage into empty space.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Reset and repeat">
    If the first trade does not finish the target, disengage, heal, or reposition. Winning a round usually means staying composed, not being greedy.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Match phase | What you should do | What you should avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Spawn | Read the roll and move to space | Dashing in blind |
| Mid-fight | Bait cooldowns and look for setup | Spamming burst early |
| Execute | Convert control into damage | Chasing a running target too long |
| Recovery | Heal or reposition | Fighting while your tools are down |

<Callout type="tip" title="Tempo Rule">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Counters, Common Errors, and FAQ">

A Gojo-style round plan falls apart when you waste your setup or ignore your defensive tools. Most mistakes are not about bad aim; they are about bad timing. If you learn the three or four most common failure points, you will improve faster than players who simply spam their strongest move and hope the lobby cooperates.

| Problem | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Missing **Hollow Purple** | You charged before creating space | Use stun or pressure first |
| Dying with **Simple Domain** unused | You saved it too long | Treat it as a reaction tool |
| Losing to rushdowns | You stood still too long | Add mobility to your plan |
| Wasting a domain | You used it on one moving target | Save it for clustered fights |
| Low Yen progression | You skipped the code step | Redeem codes before grinding |

**Counter checklist**

- Keep moving until you confirm your opening.
- Use a stun or teleport before your biggest damage.
- Hold defense for burst windows, not random chip damage.
- Leave bad fights instead of donating cooldowns.
- Spend Yen only after you know what you actually want.

<Callout type="warning" title="What to Avoid">
Do not treat the strongest-themed look as a guarantee of strength. In a random-skill arena, bad timing loses more fights than weak cosmetics ever will.
</Callout>
