<Section icon={Swords} title="Jujutsu Randomizer Skills and Fight Planning">

The strongest players do not memorize one combo; they learn how each roll fits into a fight. In Jujutsu Randomizer, high-value kits usually combine burst, crowd control, movement, or a defensive escape. That makes quick recognition more important than raw button mashing.

<Callout type="info" title="Skill Priority">
Look for one of four things on every roll: **damage**, **mobility**, **control**, or **survival**. If your kit has two of those, you can usually play the round with confidence.
</Callout>

| Skill type | Best use | Risk level |
|---|---|---|
| Burst damage | Finish low-health targets | Medium |
| Mobility | Reposition, chase, or escape | Low |
| Control | Freeze, stun, knockback, or slow | Low to Medium |
| Domain pressure | Win crowded fights | Medium |
| Self-cost damage | Trade health for stronger output | High |

| Practical skill cue | What it means | Your response |
|---|---|---|
| Fast dash or teleport | You can force openings | Use it to start fights or disengage safely |
| Freeze or stun | You can land a follow-up hit | Save your biggest damage for after the lock |
| Area damage | You can punish clusters | Use it when players stack too close |
| Healing or invulnerability | You can recover mistakes | Reset spacing before re-engaging |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify your opener">
    Check whether your first reliable action is a dash, stun, projectile, or counter. Your opener decides how you should start the round.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Find one follow-up">
    Pair your opener with a second skill that actually ends the trade. A stun without damage is just a polite suggestion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Keep one escape tool ready">
    Save a movement or defense skill for panic moments. Spending everything on offense usually turns a good roll into a short life.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Use domains with intent">
    Do not waste a big arena tool on a single enemy who can simply leave. Domains are strongest when enemies are trapped or distracted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="High-Risk Rolls">
Skills that cost HP or punish bad timing can be excellent, but they are rough for beginners. Use them only when the damage trade is clearly worth it.
</Callout>

| Common fight pattern | Example effect | Best answer |
|---|---|---|
| Control into burst | Freeze, then heavy hit | Use one setup tool before your finisher |
| Mobility into pressure | Dash, swap, or teleport | Keep chasing, but do not overcommit |
| Area denial | Domain or large explosion | Back out and re-enter on your terms |
| Panic reset | Heal or invulnerability | Disengage, recover, and reset spacing |
